It all started with increase in Dropouts rate in India Here are some reason

Dropouts in India are a complex issue influenced by various socio-economic and cultural factors. One primary reason for high dropout rates is poverty. Many families struggle to afford education-related expenses, leading children to abandon school in pursuit of income-generating opportunities. Additionally, inadequate infrastructure, especially in rural areas, results in a lack of access to quality education. Social norms and gender bias also play a significant role, with girls often facing discrimination and early marriage, limiting their educational prospects. Furthermore, a rigid and outdated curriculum, along with a lack of motivation and engagement, fails to keep students interested in their studies. Addressing these multifaceted challenges is essential to reducing dropout rates and ensuring a brighter future for India's youth.

The use of the Date from all Institution to moniter dropouts rates Why there should be a monitoring system

Creating a systematic monitoring system for dropout rates in India is imperative for several reasons. Firstly, it provides crucial insights into the effectiveness of educational policies and programs, enabling policymakers to make informed decisions to improve the quality and accessibility of education. Secondly, it helps identify regions and demographics with higher dropout rates, allowing targeted interventions to address disparities and ensure equitable educational opportunities. Additionally, tracking dropout rates facilitates early intervention strategies, such as mentoring and support programs, to retain students in the education system, ultimately contributing to a more skilled and productive workforce. Ultimately, such a system aids in fostering a more inclusive and prosperous society by reducing educational inequalities and enhancing the overall educational landscape in India.
